Meet the Celestials


SUN
It is a star at the center of the Solar System.
Composition: Hydrogen & Helium
Radius: 6,95,842 km


MERCURY
Mercury is both the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the sun.
Orbital period: 87.97 days
Radius: 2,439.7 km
Moons: 0


VENUS
Venus is the closest planet to Earth and one of the brightest objects in the sky. Referred to as Earth's 'sister planet' due to similar size, mass, composition, and proximity to the sun.
Orbital period: 225 days
Radius: 6,051.8 km
Moons: 0


EARTH
Earth is the third planet from the sun, and the only place we know of so far that’s inhabited by living things.
Orbital period: 365.26 days
Radius: 6,371 km
Moons: 1


MARS
Mars is a dusty, cold, desert world with a thin atmosphere. It has seasons, polar ice caps, extinct volcanoes, canyons, and weather.
Orbital period: 686.98 days
Radius: 3,389.5 km
Moons: 2
Referred as "The Red Planet"


JUPITER
Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. After the Moon and Venus, it's usually the next brightest object in the night sky.
Orbital period: 11.86 years
Radius: 69,911 km
Moons: 79


SATURN
Saturn, the sixth planet from the sun, is the second-largest planet in our solar system by mass and size.
Orbital period 29.44 years
Radius: 58,232 km
Moons: 82
“The Jewel of the Solar System."


URANUS
Uranus spins on its side as it orbits the sun.
Orbital period: 83.96 years
Radius: 25,362 km
Moons: 27


NEPTUNE
Neptune is the farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. It is the densest giant planet.
Orbital period 164.77 years
Radius: 24,622 km
Moons: 14
